About The Summit

The Ogoni Entrepreneurship Summit (OES) is a strategic initiative championing mentorship, entrepreneurship, and skills development to revive leadership continuity in Ogoniland. Conceived by Chief Lesi Maol and inspired by the Nnewi wealth creation model, OES bridges the gap between established professionals and aspiring youths, creating a structured platform for economic empowerment and sustainable growth.

The Summit Experience

Join us for three days of inspiring keynotes, hands-on workshops, networking opportunities, and unforgettable experiences that will shape the future of our community and beyond.

Our Vision

To build a resilient, empowered, and economically vibrant Ogoni population driven through transgenerational mentorship, entrepreneurship, innovation, and professional excellence.

Our Mission

  • To provide high-quality mentorship to aspiring Ogonis.
  • To equip participants with business, career, and digital skills.
  • To promote investment opportunities and market access.

Expected Outcomes

  • Strengthened networks across generations.
  • Improved access to business knowledge and finance.
  • Foundation for a long-term Ogoni mentorship ecosystem.

Meet the Convener

Chief Lesi Maol

Summit Convener

Chief Lesi Maol is an Ogoni statesman, industrialist, and philanthropist whose lifeâ€™s work reflects a deep commitment to the social and economic transformation of his people. Renowned for his clarity of vision, strategic leadership, and unwavering dedication to community development, Chief Maol has consistently used his influence, resources, and networks to promote empowerment, enterprise, and sustainable progress across Ogoniland.

Raised with strong values of diligence, service, and integrity, he built a successful career in business through perseverance, innovation, and a keen understanding of emerging opportunities. Over the years, he has established himself as a formidable figure in the industrial and commercial sectors, playing a pivotal role in creating jobs, supporting small-scale entrepreneurs, and modelling the possibilities that lie within disciplined enterprise.

His interest in mentorship and structured empowerment is strongly shaped by his belief that the future of Ogoni depends on restoring systems of guidance, apprenticeship, and intergenerational support. Drawing inspiration from successful community models such as the Nnewi enterprise culture, he is championing a new pathway through the Ogoni Entrepreneurship Summit, designed to nurture a self-sustaining ecosystem of entrepreneurs, professionals, and innovators.
